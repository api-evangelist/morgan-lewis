The DOJ has implemented an expedited review process for False Claims Act whistleblower cases involving federally funded, state-administered benefits programs, requiring initial reviews within 60-120 days. The accelerated timeline is expected to shorten seal periods, increase relator-led litigation when DOJ declines intervention, and move investigations more quickly, creating earlier litigation milestones for organizations receiving federal benefits funding.
